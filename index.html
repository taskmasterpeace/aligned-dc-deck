Your Website
Your content is great. The plumbing behind it just needs fixing. Here are the issues, explained in plain English, with exactly how to fix each one.
Google still thinks your business is called "Top Level"
There is hidden code on every page of your website -- like a phone book entry that only Google can read. Right now, that entry still says "Top Level," uses your old email (topleveldc1@gmail.com), and points to the old website address. Google is confused about who you are.
Think of it this way: You changed the sign on the building, but the lease, the phone book, and your business license all still say the old name. That is what Google sees.

The fix: Easy Your web person updates the business info in Wix settings. Takes about 30 minutes. Or, you can update your Google Business Profile yourself in 15 minutes (go to business.google.com and make sure all your info is correct).

Impact: This affects your Google search ranking, your Google Maps listing, and whether customers see the right info when they search for you.
119 blog posts Google probably cannot find
You have a huge library of helpful articles -- strain guides, DC cannabis law guides, wellness content. But the way Wix builds your website means Google often cannot actually read those articles.
Think of it this way: You wrote 119 articles, but they are locked in a filing cabinet. Google can see the cabinet but cannot always open the drawers.

The fix: Medium Submit your sitemap to Google Search Console. This gives Google a full map of all your pages and asks it to come read them. A Wix specialist can do this in about an hour.

Why it matters: Those blog posts are supposed to bring in people searching for things like "best cannabis for anxiety DC" or "dispensary near Tenleytown." If Google cannot find them, all that writing is going to waste.
Your business hours are invisible to Google
The behind-the-scenes code does not include your hours of operation. When someone Googles you, Google may not show when you are open.
Think of it this way: It is like having a shop with no sign on the door saying when you are open. People might not come because they are not sure if you are there.

The fix: Easy This can be fixed in two places: (1) Your Google Business Profile -- you can update this yourself in 10 minutes. (2) The website code -- your web person adds a few lines. About 30 minutes.
"Sound Bath Sunday" is misspelled in 8+ links
Multiple event pages have web addresses that say "south-bath-sunday" instead of "sound-bath-sunday." This affects at least 8 event links.
Think of it this way: It is like printing "Sund Bath" on your flyers -- except online, search engines remember it and it makes it harder for people to find your events when they search for "sound bath DC."

The fix: Medium Your web person changes each URL slug and Wix automatically redirects the old links. About 1-2 hours of work.
Event signups are broken (Calendly payment issue)
Sound Bath Sunday has zero signups right now -- not because people are not interested, but because the payment processing through Calendly got flagged and is not working.
Quick fix for this weekend: Easy Create a free Google Form where people can sign up, then collect payment at the door or via Venmo/Zelle. Not fancy, but it works immediately.

Better fix for next week: Medium Set up Eventbrite. Free for free events, small fee for paid tickets. Eventbrite also helps people discover your events -- your competitor YANA has 117 events on Eventbrite because that is where the DC wellness audience looks.

Bottom line: The service exists, the content is great, people want to come. The only thing broken is the door. Let us fix the door.

  • You already sell lifestyle products. Crystals, sage bundles, herbal teas. Your customers are buying your vibe, not just your product.

The Design Direction
What fits your brand:
  • Sacred geometry patterns (Flower of Life, Metatron's Cube)
  • Chakra color progressions with botanical elements

What we are NOT doing: Cartoon pot leaves, "420 Blaze It" messaging, rasta colors, or anything that looks like it belongs at a head shop. This is a wellness brand, and the apparel should reflect that.
